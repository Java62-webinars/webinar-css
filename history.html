Early Soviet Missions (1960s–1970s)

The Soviet Union was the first nation to send missions to Mars. Despite many failures, it achieved several important milestones: Mars 3 was the first spacecraft to make a soft landing on Mars in 1971, although contact was lost after 14.5 seconds.

  • Mars 1 (1962) – First flyby attempt, lost contact en route.
  • Mars 2 (1971) – First spacecraft to reach the surface (crash landing).
  • Mars 3 (1971) – First soft landing on Mars.
  • Mars 5 (1973) – Sent data from orbit.
USA

Mariner 4 – First Close Flyby (1965)

NASA's Mariner 4 was the first spacecraft to successfully fly by Mars and send back images of the Martian surface, revealing a cold, cratered planet.

Viking Missions – First Successful Landers (1976)

Viking 1 and Viking 2 were NASA's first landers to reach Mars. They returned detailed images and conducted experiments to search for signs of life.

Modern NASA Rovers

Rovers like Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, and Perseverance expanded our knowledge of Mars. They explored terrain, analyzed rocks, and found evidence of ancient water.
